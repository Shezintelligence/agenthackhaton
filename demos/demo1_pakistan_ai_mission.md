| Player | AI Spending (2025) | Key Moves |
|---|---|---|
| **United States** | ~$471B cumulative private investment; $202B VC in 2025 alone (50% of global VC) | Stargate ($500B UAE deal), export controls on GPUs, OpenAI >$100B spent |

### The Gap Is Not Closing — It Is Accelerating

- **India** is spending ~500x more on AI infrastructure than Pakistan. India's private sector alone has committed $70B in AI compute execution.
- **Saudi Arabia's single Humain project** (1GW cluster) has more compute capacity planned than Pakistan's entire national grid could support.
- **Global AI spending** will hit **$2.52 trillion by end of 2026**. Pakistan's share: effectively 0%.
- Pakistan produces **50,000+ IT graduates/year** but has no domestic compute to employ them in AI work. They will leave or work for foreign platforms at commodity wages.

## PAKISTAN'S CURRENT AI STACK — HONEST ASSESSMENT

### 1. TALENT

| Metric | Current State | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| IT workforce | ~600,000–800,000 (registered + informal) | Bulk in legacy web/mobile dev, <5% AI-skilled |
| Annual IT graduates | 50,000+ | Curriculum 3–5 years behind; <10 universities teach applied ML |
| Freelancers | ~2 million across platforms | Mostly low-skill gig work; <50,000 in AI-adjacent roles |
| PhD-level AI researchers | ~200–300 in-country | Critical shortage; most PhDs are abroad (est. 2,000–3,000 Pakistani AI PhDs globally) |
| AI trainers / data labelers | ~10,000–20,000 | Unorganized; no quality certification; could scale to 200,000+ |

**Key Insight**: Pakistan has VOLUME (young, English-speaking, digitally connected), but NOT DEPTH. The conversion from "IT worker" to "AI practitioner" requires 6–12 months of focused upskilling at scale.

### 2. COMPUTE

| Asset | Status |
|---|---|
| GPU clusters | **ONE** — Data Vault/Telenor facility in Karachi (launched Dec 2025). Small-scale. |
| Enterprise GPU access | Pakistan is on **Nvidia's restricted export list**. Cannot legally purchase H100/B200 at scale without special compliance waivers. |
| Cloud infrastructure | No hyperscaler region (no AWS, Azure, GCP local zone). All compute routed through Singapore/Mumbai. Latency: 80–150ms. |
| HPC | NCP (National Centre for Physics) has limited HPC. Not AI-grade. |
| National data centers | Handful of Tier-II/III commercial facilities. None AI-optimized. |

**Critical Dependency**: Pakistan has **ZERO sovereign AI compute at meaningful scale**. Everything depends on foreign cloud. This is the single biggest bottleneck.

### 3. DATA

| Domain | Data Availability | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| Population (NADRA) | 230M+ biometric records | Good structure, restricted access |
| Tax (FBR) | Transaction-level data, growing | Fragmented, partially digitized |
| Health | Provincial systems, mostly paper | Very poor digitization |
| Agriculture | Limited satellite + crop data | Unstructured, incomplete |
| Telecom | 190M+ mobile users, usage data at telcos | Privately held, no sharing framework |
| Language/NLP | Urdu corpus exists but limited; regional languages (Sindhi, Pashto, Punjabi) almost absent | Major gap for LLM localization |

**Critical Gap**: No **National Data Framework** — no data sharing protocols, no anonymization standards, no data governance law. Without this, AI models cannot be trained on Pakistani data.

### 4. INSTITUTIONS

| Institution | Role | AI Readiness |
|---|---|---|
| MoIT&T | Policy lead | AI Policy 2025 approved; execution machinery absent |
| NITB | E-governance | Some digital systems; no AI capability |
| HEC | Higher education | Slow; curriculum reform pending |
| SMEDA | SME support | No AI programs |
| NESCOM/strategic orgs | Defense/strategic tech | Classified; assumed basic ML capability |
| Provincial IT boards (PITB, SiTB, KPITB) | Provincial digital services | Punjab PITB most advanced; others weak |

**Critical Gap**: No single empowered body owns AI execution. The National AI Policy 2025 created an "AI Council" on paper but it has no budget, no staff, and no authority as of Feb 2026.

### 5. POLICY & LEGAL

- **National AI Policy 2025**: Approved August 2025. Six pillars. Good framework. **Zero execution funding allocated.**
- **Data Protection**: No federal data protection law. Draft exists since 2023, not passed.
- **IP/AI ownership**: No legal clarity on AI-generated content, liability, or autonomous decisions.
- **Procurement**: Government procurement rules (~PPRA) prevent rapid tech acquisition. 6–18 month cycles.
- **Startup ecosystem**: Some progress (NSTP, NIC). But no AI-specific incubation with compute access.

### FOREIGN DEPENDENCIES — RED FLAGS

1. **Compute**: 100% dependent on foreign cloud (mostly routed through India/Singapore)
2. **GPUs**: Cannot buy Nvidia enterprise chips legally without US compliance waiver
3. **LLMs**: All major models (GPT, Claude, Gemini, Llama) controlled by US companies
4. **Payment rails**: Freelancers depend on Payoneer/PayPal (limited); no sovereign digital payment for AI services
5. **Submarine cables**: Pakistan's internet bandwidth dependent on 4–5 submarine cables; no redundancy for AI-scale data transfer
