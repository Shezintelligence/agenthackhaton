## 1.1 Global Regulatory Landscape (as of Feb 2026)

### United States
- **GENIUS Act** (signed July 18, 2025): First comprehensive federal stablecoin legislation â€” mandates reserves, audits, financial integrity for stablecoin issuers.
- SEC placed Bitcoin & Ethereum ETFs under generic listing standard (Oct 2025). US spot Bitcoin ETFs managing **$150â€“170B in assets** by late Sep 2025.
- DTC tokenization pilot (no-action letter Dec 2025) launching H2 2026.
- Regulatory posture: **Pro-institutional, pro-stablecoin, rapidly maturing.**

### European Union (MiCA)
- Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation took **full effect Jan 2025**.
- **40+ CASP licenses issued** across EU by mid-2025 (Netherlands, Malta, Germany leading).
- EU crypto market estimated at **â‚¬1.5â€“1.8 trillion** by end of 2025.
- 18-month transition for some member states (France, Malta, Luxembourg through June 2026).
- Posture: **Comprehensive, compliance-heavy, institutional-grade framework. Global gold standard.**

### UAE / Dubai (VARA)
- VARA: World's first dedicated Virtual Assets regulatory authority. Operational since 2023.
- **DFSA updated crypto token rules effective Jan 12, 2026.**
- New SCA framework for VASPs expected **finalized early 2026**.
- CARF (crypto-asset reporting framework) rules expected 2026, exchange of info from 2028.
- Posture: **Aggressive first-mover, attracting global talent & capital. Direct competitor to Pakistan.**

### Singapore (MAS)
- **FSMA licensing mandatory for all crypto service providers by June 30, 2025** â€” including those serving only overseas clients.
- **33 MAS-licensed companies** as of 2025.
- No capital gains tax on crypto investments.
- Posture: **Institutional-quality, selective, zero tax on investment gains. Premium jurisdiction.**

### India
- **30% flat tax** on crypto gains + **1% TDS** on all transactions maintained into FY 2026-27.
- New â‚¹200/day penalties and â‚¹50,000 fines for non-reporting from April 2026.
- **#1 in Chainalysis Global Crypto Adoption Index 2025.**
- No comprehensive crypto bill passed, operating through tax code + SEBI oversight.
- Posture: **Tolerated but punitively taxed. Massive adoption despite hostile tax regime.**

### Hong Kong
- SFC licensing regime for Virtual Asset Trading Platforms (VATPs) operational.
- Retail access permitted for licensed platforms.
- Posture: **Open for business, bridging China-global crypto corridor.**

### Saudi Arabia
- **No crypto law. Effectively prohibited** â€” SAMA warns against dealing.
- However, pivoting toward **tokenization, CBDC pilots, and stablecoin frameworks** under Vision 2030.
- Joined mBridge CBDC pilot (with UAE, China, Thailand, Hong Kong).
- Posture: **Cautiously exploring institutional use; retail crypto remains banned.**

## 1.2 Where Pakistan Stands (Feb 2026)

- **#3 globally in Chainalysis Crypto Adoption Index 2025** (after India and US).
- **~15.9 million crypto users** (~6.6% ownership rate).
- **Pakistan Crypto Council (PCC)** established March 14, 2025; chaired by Finance Minister Aurangzeb.
- **PVARA (Pakistan Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority)** established July 8, 2025 via ordinance.
- Bilal Bin Saqib appointed Chairman of PVARA and Minister of State for Crypto & Blockchain.
- **PVARA issued NOCs to Binance and HTX** on December 12, 2025 (not full licenses â€” preliminary clearances).
- Ordinance **valid for 120 days, extended by Senate** â€” but NOT yet passed as permanent Act of Parliament.
- **No domestic exchange has received a full operational license** as of Feb 2026.
- MoU with Binance Investments for tokenization of sovereign assets (bonds, treasury bills, commodities).
- Regulatory sandbox framework exists in the ordinance but **not yet operationalized**.
- Sharia Advisory Committee mandated but **composition and functioning unclear**.

**CRITICAL STATUS: Pakistan has momentum and institutional scaffolding but is operating on an ordinance (temporary law) and has zero licensed domestic VASPs. The gap between ambition and operational reality is significant.**

## 2.3 Infrastructure Assessment

- **Banking penetration**: Only ~30% of adults have formal bank accounts. JazzCash/Easypaisa mobile wallets are the bridge.
- **Internet penetration**: ~55% (~130M users), mobile-first. 4G dominant. Sufficient for crypto access but not for high-frequency institutional trading.
- **Power grid**: Pakistan has surplus power capacity; PM himself invited Bitcoin miners (Reuters, Apr 2025). Potential for mining infrastructure.
- **Payment infrastructure**: Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) via SBP. RAAST instant payment system operational. Integration point for crypto on/off ramps.

## 2.4 Critical Dependencies

1. **Parliamentary action**: PVARA ordinance MUST become Act. Without this, everything collapses.
2. **FBR engagement**: Tax framework must exist before exchanges can operate legally (reporting, withholding, compliance).
3. **SBP banking bridge**: Banks need explicit SBP permission to open accounts for licensed VASPs. Currently a prohibition mindset.
4. **International technical assistance**: Need support from FATF, IMF (already in EFF program), and bilateral regulatory exchanges.
5. **Bilal Bin Saqib continuity**: Current framework heavily dependent on a single champion. Institutional resilience is thin.

## 3.2 Security Threat Assessment

### Money Laundering (ML)

| Threat | Current Level | Post-Regulation Level | Notes |
|--------|--------------|----------------------|-------|
| Hawala-crypto hybrid networks | **SEVERE** | Medium-High | Current P2P and informal channels are invisible. Regulation brings KYC/AML to formal exchanges, but informal channels will persist. |

| Layering via international exchanges | HIGH | Medium | NOCs to Binance/HTX with AML requirements will help. Cross-border MoUs needed. |

### Terrorism Financing (TF)

| Threat | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Senate committee (Sep 2025) explicitly flagged crypto for TF risk. Pakistan's history on FATF grey list makes this **non-negotiable**. |
| **Mitigation**: PVARA ordinance mandates FATF Recommendation 15 compliance. Travel rule implementation, transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting (STR) integration with Financial Monitoring Unit (FMU) are essential. |
| **Timeline risk**: If AML/CFT implementing rules are not published within 6 months, FATF may flag this in next mutual evaluation cycle. |

### Consumer Scams

| Threat | Current Level | Notes |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| Ponzi/pyramid schemes | **SEVERE** | $100M FIA scam case (2022) just one example. Social media-driven schemes targeting youth and low-income users. |
| Exchange hacks/theft | HIGH | No insurance requirements, no custody standards. |
| Rug pulls (DeFi) | HIGH | DEX access uncontrollable; consumer education is the only tool. |

### Capital Flight

| Concern | Assessment |
|---------|------------|
| SBP and FBR fear that regulated crypto = capital flight channel. This is the **primary institutional resistance point**. |
| **Counter-argument**: Capital flight is already happening via unregulated channels. Regulation provides visibility, not enablement. Regulated exchanges with transaction limits, KYC, and reporting actually restrict capital flight relative to current P2P/hawala status quo. |
| **Data needed**: SBP should commission study on estimated informal crypto capital flows vs formal banking channels. |

## 4.2 Risks: Too Fast vs Too Slow

### Moving Too Fast
- Licensing without adequate AML/CFT infrastructure = FATF grey list risk
- Consumer losses from premature market opening without protection rules
- Binance NOC without due diligence completion could backfire if Binance faces new global enforcement
- Sovereign tokenization before legal clarity could create constitutional/legal challenges

### Moving Too Slow
- Ordinance expires (has a hard deadline)
- Talent drain accelerates (every month of delay = developers moving to UAE)
- India's punitive tax regime may change (India could become friendlier), closing Pakistan's window
- Informal economy continues to grow, making eventual regulation harder
- Pakistan loses #3 adoption ranking narrative advantage

**Assessment: The balance should tilt toward FAST but SEQUENCED. The ordinance deadline creates an artificial but real urgency. Speed is needed on the legislative track; caution is needed on licensing and market opening.**
